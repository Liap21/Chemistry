A History of the Atom

Theories and Models



Ernest Rutherford

(1911)

Rutherford fired positively charged aplha particles at a thin sheet of gold foil. Most passed through with little deflection, some deflected at large angles. This was only possible if the atom was mostly empty space, with the positive charge concentrated in the centre: the nucleus.

Niels Bohr

(1913)

Bohr modified Rutherford's model of the atom by stating that electrons moved around the nucleus in orbits of fixed sizes and energies. Electron energy in this model was quantised; electrons could not occupy values of energy between the fixed energy levels.

Erwin Schrodinger

(1926)

Schrodinger stated that electrons do not move in set paths around the nucleus, but in waves. It is impossible to know the exact location of the electrons;instead, we have 'clouds of probability' called orbitals, in which we are more likely to find an electron
